Talakona waterfalls

Talakona Waterfall, located in Sri Venkateswara National Park in Andhra Pradesh, is the state's highest waterfall, with a spectacular 270-foot drop into a pool surrounded by lush forests. Visitors can trek to the site through well-marked trails and enjoy the tranquil environment and diverse flora and fauna, while the water is believed by some to have medicinal properties. The "head of the hill" in Telugu, Talakona is a popular destination for nature lovers, trekkers, and eco-tourism, though visitors should be prepared with comfortable gear and be mindful of the surroundings
