Badrinath Temple

The Badrinath Temple is a revered Hindu shrine in Uttarakhand, India, dedicated to Lord Vishnu and a key destination on the Char Dham pilgrimage route. Nestled in the Himalayas, its spiritual significance is immense, drawing pilgrims for its sacred atmosphere, the chance to bathe in the holy Alaknanda River, and the presence of a black stone idol of Lord Vishnu. The temple, with its distinctive gold-gilt roof and main entrance, the Singhdwar, was established by Adi Shankaracharya in the 8th century and remains open for worship from roughly April to November.
