Dakshineswar Kali Temple

The Dakshineswar Kali Temple is a deeply revered and architecturally significant Hindu temple on the banks of the Hooghly River, dedicated to Goddess Kali. Founded by Rani Rashmoni in 1855, it features traditional Nava-ratna (nine-spired) architecture and is famously associated with the mystical insights and spiritual teachings of Ramakrishna Paramhansa. The complex also includes shrines for Lord Shiva and other deities, making it a major pilgrimage site and an important cultural landmark in Kolkata.
