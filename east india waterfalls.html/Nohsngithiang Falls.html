Nohsngithiang Falls

Noh Sngithiang Falls, also known as the Seven Sisters Falls, is a prominent waterfall in the Sohra (Cherrapunjee) region of Meghalaya, India, notable for its unique segmented structure where seven streams fall from the top of a cliff. The falls are seasonal, flowing primarily during the rainy season when they are at their most spectacular, creating a curtain of water and a perennial rainbow. A visit during this period offers the best views from viewpoints in Mawsmai village.
