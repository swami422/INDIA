Dwarkadhish Temple

The Dwarkadhish Temple, known as the Jagat Mandir, is a significant Hindu pilgrimage site in Gujarat, dedicated to Lord Krishna. It is a part of the sacred Char Dham pilgrimage, built on the site of Krishna's ancient city of Dwarka, which legends say was lost to the sea. The temple is renowned for its intricate architecture, a towering shikhara (spire), and daily rituals, including the dramatic changing of the temple's flag three times a day.
